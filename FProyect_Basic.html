What is cellulose?

Cellulose is a very common organic compound in nature, as it is found in almost all organic matter, such as leaves, grass and tree bark. This compound is incredibly important to our daily life, as it has been used as raw material for the entire paper industry and its derivatives. Problem behind cellulose

The great environmental problem behind cellulose is that 75% of all trees that are being cut down in the world are destined for the extraction of cellulose for the paper industry. We are talking about a lost of forest area at a speed of 30 football fields per minute .
This is huge and incredibly costly to the environment and the planet. Trees are the natural hero fighting global warming.
    These incredible beings are capable of:
  • Absorbing carbon dioxide from the atmosphere.
  • Releasing 60% of the oxygen in the planet.
  • Filtering the air and underground water.
  • Many other important things...
As you can see, we can not afford to continue consuming our forests with such unsustainable processes.
